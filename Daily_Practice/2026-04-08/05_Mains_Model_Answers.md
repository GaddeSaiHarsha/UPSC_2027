**Question: Evaluate the role of India's Multi-Hazard Early Warning System in transforming disaster management and its implications for sustainable development. (15 marks)**

India's Multi-Hazard Early Warning Decision Support System (MHEW-DSS) marks a revolutionary approach to disaster management, transitioning from reactive response to proactive preparedness through integrated technological solutions.

**Technological Integration and Scope**: The system leverages artificial intelligence, machine learning, and satellite technology to provide comprehensive warnings for multiple hazards including cyclones, floods, heatwaves, droughts, and landslides. This multi-hazard approach addresses India's vulnerability to diverse natural disasters, moving beyond traditional single-hazard warning systems.

**Constitutional and Legal Framework**: The initiative strengthens Article 21 (Right to Life) by ensuring citizens receive timely disaster warnings. It operationalizes the Disaster Management Act 2005 and aligns with NDMA guidelines, creating a robust legal foundation for early warning systems.

**Governance and Implementation**: The system enhances coordination between central agencies like IMD, NDMA, and state disaster management authorities. It supports the federal structure by enabling state-specific customization while maintaining national coordination, addressing the concurrent nature of disaster management under the Seventh Schedule.

**Sustainable Development Impact**: By reducing disaster-induced economic losses and protecting vulnerable populations, MHEW-DSS directly contributes to SDG 1 (No Poverty), SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities), and SDG 13 (Climate Action). The system's focus on rural and remote areas ensures inclusive development.

India's MHEW-DSS represents a paradigmatic shift toward anticipatory governance, positioning the nation as a global leader in disaster risk reduction while building climate-resilient communities essential for sustainable development.
