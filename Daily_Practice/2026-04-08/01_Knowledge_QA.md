**Question: Analyze the significance of India's Multi-Hazard Early Warning System in strengthening disaster preparedness. (15 marks)**

India's Multi-Hazard Early Warning Decision Support System (MHEW-DSS), launched in January 2024 under Mission Mausam, represents a paradigm shift in disaster management. Unlike traditional single-hazard systems, it provides integrated warnings for cyclones, floods, heatwaves, droughts, and landslides through advanced AI and machine learning.

The system aligns with Article 21 (Right to Life) by ensuring timely warnings reach vulnerable populations. It supports the Sendai Framework's disaster risk reduction goals and complements existing mechanisms like the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) guidelines. The integration with local administration ensures last-mile connectivity, crucial for India's diverse geographical challenges.

Key benefits include reduced response time, better resource allocation, and enhanced community preparedness. The system's multi-lingual capabilities address India's linguistic diversity, while its integration with mobile networks ensures widespread reach. This technological advancement positions India as a leader in disaster preparedness, potentially reducing economic losses and saving lives through proactive rather than reactive approaches.
