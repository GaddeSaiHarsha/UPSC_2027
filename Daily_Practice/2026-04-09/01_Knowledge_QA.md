India's increased contribution to the Green Climate Fund represents a strategic diplomatic move that balances climate leadership with advocacy for climate justice. This contribution demonstrates India's commitment to South-South cooperation while simultaneously pressuring developed nations to fulfill their climate finance obligations under the Paris Agreement's Common but Differentiated Responsibilities and Respective Capabilities (CBDR-RC) principle.

The move aligns with India's constitutional directive under Article 51 to promote international peace and cooperation. It strengthens India's negotiating position in climate forums by showcasing moral leadership while maintaining the stance that developed nations, as historical emitters, must bear primary responsibility for climate finance. This contribution also supports India's broader climate diplomacy, including initiatives like the International Solar Alliance and Coalition for Disaster Resilient Infrastructure.

However, India continues to emphasize that its contribution doesn't absolve developed nations of their $100 billion annual commitment. This strategic approach enhances India's soft power while advocating for equitable climate action that considers developmental needs of emerging economies.
