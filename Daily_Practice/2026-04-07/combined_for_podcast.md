**Question: Analyze the significance of India's new cyber resilience framework for critical information infrastructure and examine the challenges in its implementation.**

India's new cyber resilience framework addresses the growing vulnerabilities in critical information infrastructure (CII) amid increasing digitalization. The framework builds upon the IT Act 2000 and CERT-In guidelines, recognizing that existing laws need enhancement for modern threats.

Key significance includes establishing sector-specific security standards for banking, power, and telecommunications, mandatory incident reporting mechanisms, and public-private partnerships for threat intelligence sharing. The framework aligns with the National Cyber Security Strategy 2020 and supports the Digital India mission's security imperatives.

Implementation challenges encompass capacity building across diverse sectors, ensuring compliance without hampering innovation, and coordinating between multiple agencies like NCIIPC, CERT-In, and sectoral regulators. Resource constraints, especially for smaller operators, and the rapidly evolving threat landscape pose additional hurdles.

The framework's success depends on regular updates, industry collaboration, and integration with international cybersecurity protocols, making it crucial for India's digital sovereignty and economic security.
