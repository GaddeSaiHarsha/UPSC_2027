**Question: Critically examine India's new framework for cyber resilience of critical information infrastructure and its implications for national security.**

India's unveiling of a comprehensive cyber resilience framework represents a paradigmatic shift from reactive to proactive cybersecurity governance, addressing vulnerabilities in sectors vital to national functioning and economic stability.

**Strategic Significance**: The framework operationalizes Article 21's right to life by protecting essential services, while supporting Article 19's digital rights through secure infrastructure. It builds upon the IT Act 2000, CERT-In guidelines, and the National Cyber Security Strategy 2020, creating a unified approach to critical infrastructure protection across banking, power, telecommunications, and transportation sectors.

**Institutional Architecture**: The framework strengthens existing institutions like NCIIPC and CERT-In while establishing sector-specific Computer Emergency Response Teams (sectoral CERTs). It mandates threat intelligence sharing, incident reporting protocols, and regular security audits, creating a multi-layered defense ecosystem that involves both government and private stakeholders.

**Implementation Mechanisms**: Key features include mandatory cybersecurity frameworks for CII operators, real-time threat monitoring systems, and standardized incident response procedures. The framework emphasizes public-private partnerships, recognizing that over 90% of critical infrastructure is privately owned, necessitating collaborative governance models.

**Challenges and Limitations**: Implementation faces hurdles including capacity constraints, compliance costs for smaller operators, coordination complexities between multiple agencies, and the dynamic nature of cyber threats. The framework must balance security imperatives with innovation and ease of doing business.

The framework positions India strategically in an era where cyber warfare increasingly targets civilian infrastructure, making cyber resilience integral to comprehensive national security and digital sovereignty in the 21st century.
