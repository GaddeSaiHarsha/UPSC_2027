**Question: Analyze the significance of India's unified digital identity framework beyond Aadhaar and examine its implications for governance and citizen rights.**

India's move toward a unified digital identity framework represents a paradigm shift in digital governance, building upon the foundational success of Aadhaar while addressing its limitations. This comprehensive system aims to integrate multiple digital identities—Aadhaar, DigiLocker, UPI, and sectoral IDs—creating an interoperable ecosystem that enhances service delivery efficiency.

The framework aligns with Article 21's right to life and dignity by ensuring seamless access to essential services, while supporting the Digital India mission's objectives. However, it raises critical concerns regarding data privacy and surveillance, necessitating robust implementation of the Personal Data Protection Act provisions.

The Justice Srikrishna Committee's recommendations on data localization and consent mechanisms become crucial here. While the system promises to reduce bureaucratic bottlenecks and enable targeted welfare delivery, risks of algorithmic bias and digital exclusion of marginalized communities must be addressed through inclusive design and strong grievance redressal mechanisms, ensuring technology serves as an enabler rather than a barrier to constitutional rights.
