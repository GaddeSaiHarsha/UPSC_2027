**Question: Critically examine India's unified digital identity framework beyond Aadhaar and its implications for digital governance and citizen empowerment.**

India's unified digital identity framework represents a transformative leap in digital governance, moving beyond the singular Aadhaar model to create a comprehensive, interoperable ecosystem of digital identities that promises to revolutionize citizen-state interactions.

**Enhanced Service Delivery and Governance Efficiency**: The framework integrates multiple digital platforms—Aadhaar, DigiLocker, UPI, and sectoral identities—creating seamless access to government services. This aligns with Article 14's equality principle by ensuring uniform service delivery while supporting the Digital India mission's vision of technology-enabled governance.

**Economic Empowerment and Financial Inclusion**: By linking various digital identities, the system enables better credit scoring, financial access, and targeted subsidy delivery. The JAM (Jan Dhan-Aadhaar-Mobile) trinity gets strengthened, potentially reducing leakages and ensuring direct benefit transfers reach intended beneficiaries more effectively.

**Privacy and Surveillance Concerns**: The Justice Srikrishna Committee's warnings about data protection become paramount. Centralized identity systems risk creating surveillance infrastructure that could undermine Article 21's privacy dimensions, as established in the Puttaswamy judgment. Robust consent mechanisms and data minimization principles are essential.

**Digital Divide and Exclusion Risks**: The framework must address concerns raised by civil society about excluding marginalized communities who lack digital literacy or access. The system needs fail-safe mechanisms ensuring no citizen is denied services due to technological barriers, maintaining the constitutional promise of inclusive development.

Moving forward, India must balance innovation with constitutional values, ensuring this digital identity ecosystem enhances rather than compromises democratic governance and individual rights.
